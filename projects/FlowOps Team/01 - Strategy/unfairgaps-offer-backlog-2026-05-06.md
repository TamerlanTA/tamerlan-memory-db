## Summary

FlowOps should not stay limited to three generic offers. The better model is a menu of **regulatory-risk automation sprints**: narrow, evidence-backed cleanup projects where businesses already lose money through fines, lawsuits, missed leads, manual follow-up, bad records, or unsafe tool stacks.

The strongest near-term pattern remains [[validation-hipaa-safe-intake-automation-2026-05-06|HIPAA-safe healthcare intake/follow-up cleanup]]. The next best candidates are listed below as a validation backlog.

## Offer candidates

### 1. HIPAA-Safe Intake + Follow-up Cleanup Sprint

**Status:** PROMISING, already validated in separate note.

**Buyer:** small clinics, therapy practices, med spas, dental, behavioral health, wellness operators.

**What FlowOps sells:** map intake/data flows, clean risky forms/trackers/chat/CRM handoffs, rebuild fast response + qualification + booking + CRM update around BAA-capable tools.

**Entry price:** $300-$750 audit, $1,500-$4,000 sprint, $500-$1,500/month support.

**Why it fits FlowOps:** directly reuses Speed-to-Lead, AI Chatbot, CRM, Pipeline C, Loom audits, and automation sprint delivery.

## 2. Consent Evidence Chain Sprint for Lead Buyers

**Status:** PROMISING, but needs deeper validation because the FCC one-to-one consent rule was vacated in 2025.

**Buyer:** home services, solar, roofing, insurance, mortgage, real estate, agencies buying leads from third-party lead vendors.

**Unfairgap:** lead buyers often cannot prove how a lead consented, which vendor touched it, which disclosure was shown, and whether the outreach path matched the consent. Even when a specific new FCC rule is vacated, FTC/TCPA/DNC risk remains active.

**Evidence signals:**
- FTC 2024 Response Tree settlement alleged deceptive lead-generation sites and millions of illegal telemarketing calls.
- TCPA lead-gen market remains confused after the one-to-one consent rule was vacated.

**Product sketch:** build a consent-proof workflow: lead-source intake, screenshot/archive of landing-page disclosure, timestamped consent fields, vendor chain log, DNC suppression check, CRM consent badge, outbound gating, and complaint lookup trail.

**Entry price:** $500-$1,000 audit, $2,000-$6,000 sprint, $750-$2,000/month monitoring.

**Risk:** buyer may see this as legal/compliance work. Position as operational evidence and vendor hygiene, not legal clearance.

## 3. OSHA Training + Safety Evidence Vault for Contractors

**Status:** PROMISING for construction and especially stone/countertop fabrication.

**Buyer:** roofing contractors, general contractors, countertop/stone fabricators, subcontractor-heavy construction firms.

**Unfairgap:** companies are often not just missing safety action; they are missing the evidence chain: training records, exposure plans, inspection logs, incident photos, toolbox talks, medical surveillance reminders, and abatement proof.

**Evidence signals:**
- OSHA 2024 Florenza Marble & Granite case proposed over $1M in penalties after silica exposure and missing safety/monitoring controls.
- OSHA/fall-protection repeat violations remain a recurring construction enforcement pattern.

**Product sketch:** Airtable/Notion + mobile form + Telegram/SMS reminder system for safety training, toolbox talks, photo evidence, PPE checks, silica exposure plan tasks, and abatement deadlines.

**Entry price:** $1,500-$5,000 sprint, $500-$2,000/month support.

**Risk:** safety consultants and EHS platforms already exist. FlowOps should sell quick evidence-chain implementation for small operators, not expert safety consulting.

## 4. ADA Website Demand-Letter Defense Prep Sprint

**Status:** PROMISING but crowded.

**Buyer:** Shopify/e-commerce stores, local service websites, restaurants, clinics, retail businesses with physical locations and weak websites.

**Unfairgap:** many small businesses rely on templates, old sites, or accessibility overlays, then get demand letters or lawsuits without a remediation log, accessibility statement, issue intake process, or WCAG fix queue.

**Evidence signals:**
- Seyfarth reported 2,452 federal website accessibility lawsuits in 2024.
- EcomBack reported 983 ADA website accessibility lawsuits in Q1 2025.

**Product sketch:** automated accessibility scan + manual review queue + WCAG fix backlog + accessibility statement + monthly regression monitor + before/after evidence report.

**Entry price:** $300-$750 audit, $1,000-$3,000 remediation sprint, $200-$800/month monitoring.

**Risk:** very saturated; lots of low-quality scanners and agencies. Best angle is "remediation evidence pack" for SMBs, not generic ADA compliance.

## 5. Auto Dealer Price/Fees Cleanup Sprint

**Status:** EMERGING/PROMISING.

**Buyer:** small and mid-size auto dealers, especially used-car dealerships and multi-location local groups.

**Unfairgap:** dealer websites, CRM templates, ad feeds, and sales handoffs often fail to keep advertised price, mandatory fees, rebates, add-ons, and final quote evidence aligned.

**Evidence signals:**
- FTC CARS Rule targeted bait-and-switch tactics and hidden junk fees.
- FTC and state AGs have pursued dealership junk-fee/deceptive-pricing cases; Rhode Island AG announced over $1M settlement with multiple dealerships in 2024.

**Product sketch:** inventory/ad-feed audit, advertised-price vs CRM quote comparison, add-on disclosure checklist, consent log for optional products, quote-template cleanup, sales manager alert when price/fee fields conflict.

**Entry price:** $750-$1,500 audit, $3,000-$8,000 sprint, $1,000-$2,500/month monitoring.

**Risk:** dealers may resist transparency because hidden fees can be intentional margin. Target dealers who want to avoid regulatory heat and compete as transparent operators.

## 6. AI Hiring Audit Trail Sprint

**Status:** EMERGING; good content/demo wedge, but probably not fastest cash.

**Buyer:** recruiting agencies, staffing firms, HR teams using AI resume screening, assessment tools, or automated candidate ranking.

**Unfairgap:** companies adopt AI screening without documenting job-related criteria, vendor behavior, bias audit status, applicant notices, human review, and rejection reason trails.

**Evidence signals:**
- EEOC iTutorGroup settlement: $365k over automated age-based rejection allegations.
- NYC Local Law 144 requires bias audit, public summary, and candidate notices for covered automated employment decision tools.

**Product sketch:** AI hiring workflow map, candidate notice templates, tool inventory, human-review checkpoint, rejection-reason logging, audit-ready exports, and annual review reminders.

**Entry price:** $750-$1,500 audit, $2,500-$7,500 sprint.

**Risk:** legal and HR complexity is high; buyers may need counsel. FlowOps should partner with HR/legal advisor if this becomes active.

## 7. Subscription / Cancellation Flow Audit

**Status:** WEAK-to-PROMISING, depends on state-law angle.

**Buyer:** subscription e-commerce, SaaS, paid communities, gyms/fitness, online education.

**Unfairgap:** many subscription businesses have unclear disclosures, weak consent capture, support-only cancellation, and poor refund/cancellation logs.

**Evidence signals:**
- FTC announced a Click-to-Cancel rule in 2024 and reported rising complaints, but the rule was vacated by the Eighth Circuit in 2025.
- FTC and state regulators can still pursue negative-option/dark-pattern cases through other authorities.

**Product sketch:** checkout disclosure audit, consent logging, cancellation-flow rebuild, refund/cancel dashboard, support macro cleanup, state-law matrix handoff for counsel.

**Entry price:** $500-$1,000 audit, $1,500-$5,000 sprint.

**Risk:** because the federal rule was vacated, this should not be the first wedge unless a specific state/vertical has stronger current enforcement.

## Immediate recommendation

Do not build all offers at once. Use them as a testing portfolio:

1. Run healthcare first for 7 days.
2. In parallel, build one landing/demo section called "Risk Cleanup Sprints" with 3 cards:
   - healthcare intake data-flow cleanup;
   - lead consent evidence chain;
   - ADA/accessibility remediation evidence pack.
3. Use Pipeline C to generate niche-specific audits and measure replies.
4. Promote any offer only after 1 paid audit, 5 serious replies, or 10 discovery calls.
