Best economic buyer: practice owner, operations manager, or marketing manager.

## Unfairgap

Small healthcare and wellness operators want fast intake and follow-up, but their website forms, chat widgets, ad pixels, CRM, booking, SMS, and email tools can move sensitive health data through generic systems.

The hole is not simply "slow lead response". It is:

```text
fast lead capture + unsafe data flow + no evidence of vendor/tool discipline
```

## What FlowOps sells

**HIPAA-Safe Intake + Follow-up Cleanup Sprint**

FlowOps maps the intake workflow, removes risky handoffs, and rebuilds the response path around safer tools and documented operating logic.

This is not legal advice and not a promise of HIPAA compliance. It is an operational cleanup and evidence sprint.

## Audit criteria

Check:
- Does the site collect health details before a relationship is established?
- Are trackers, pixels, chat widgets, or analytics tools present on intake pages?
- Is booking manual, delayed, or handled through generic forms?
- Does the site have privacy/HIPAA/BAA signals?
- Does follow-up happen through email/SMS without clear workflow control?
- Does the team copy intake data between tools manually?

## Sprint deliverables

- Intake/data-flow map.
- Tool/vendor inventory.
- Safer intake form or booking flow.
- Instant lead response workflow.
- AI qualification where appropriate.
- CRM update automation.
- Staff notification and escalation path.
- Follow-up sequence.
- BAA/vendor checklist for the client to review with counsel.
- Loom handoff and simple documentation.
