## Content

## Status

**PROMISING but crowded.** There are many scanners and accessibility agencies, so FlowOps should not sell generic "ADA compliance". The sharper wedge is an accessibility remediation evidence pack for SMBs.

## Buyer

- Shopify and WooCommerce stores;
- local service businesses;
- restaurants and hospitality;
- clinics and wellness businesses;
- retail businesses with physical locations;
- small companies with old or template-based websites.

Best economic buyer: owner, marketing manager, web/ops manager.

## Unfairgap

Many small businesses do not know whether their website is usable by people with disabilities. When they receive a demand letter or lawsuit, they often have no:
- remediation log;
- accessibility statement;
- issue intake process;
- before/after proof;
- recurring scan;
- prioritized WCAG backlog.

## What FlowOps sells

**ADA Website Defense Prep Sprint**

FlowOps scans the website, builds a fix queue, handles obvious remediations or coordinates with the web team, and gives the client an evidence pack showing what was reviewed and improved.

## Audit criteria

Check:
- missing image alt text;
- low contrast;
- keyboard navigation failures;
- unlabeled buttons/inputs;
- broken heading order;
- inaccessible forms;
- missing accessibility statement;
- no public issue contact method;
- overlay-only "solution" without real fixes.

## Sprint deliverables

- Automated scan report.
- Manual review notes for key pages.
- WCAG issue backlog.
- Priority fix plan.
- Accessibility statement draft.
- Issue intake email/form setup.
- Before/after remediation evidence.
- Monthly monitoring workflow.
- Loom handoff.

## Pricing

- Audit: **$300-$750**.
- Sprint: **$1,000-$3,000**.
- Monitoring: **$200-$800/month**.

## Outreach angle

> I ran a quick accessibility check and found a few issues that are easy to miss but risky to leave undocumented. I can turn this into a clean fix backlog and remediation evidence pack.

## Risks

- Saturated market.
- Accessibility overlays create false confidence.
- Some fixes need frontend code access.
- Must avoid promising lawsuit prevention.

## First experiment

Use Pipeline C against Shopify/local business websites.

Prioritize:
- high-traffic e-commerce;
- businesses with physical locations;
- websites with visible form/booking flows;
- old sites with obvious UI issues.

Goal:
- 50 audits;
- 5 Looms;
- 1 paid remediation sprint.
