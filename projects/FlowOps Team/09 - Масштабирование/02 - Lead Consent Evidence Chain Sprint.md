## Content

## Status

**PROMISING**, but must be positioned carefully. The FCC one-to-one consent rule was vacated in 2025, so this is not a "new rule compliance" offer. The durable wedge is operational evidence: lead buyers often cannot prove where consent came from or whether outreach matched the consent.

## Buyer

- home services companies buying leads;
- solar installers;
- roofing and HVAC companies;
- insurance and mortgage lead buyers;
- real estate teams;
- agencies that generate or resell leads.

Best economic buyer: owner, sales director, marketing director, compliance-aware ops manager.

## Unfairgap

Lead buyers and agencies often depend on third-party forms, landing pages, affiliate partners, and lead vendors. When a complaint happens, they may not have:
- exact consent timestamp;
- landing-page copy shown at the time;
- vendor/sub-vendor chain;
- DNC suppression proof;
- message/call eligibility;
- CRM history showing why outreach happened.

## What FlowOps sells

**Consent Evidence Chain Sprint**

FlowOps builds a workflow that captures and preserves proof around each lead before the sales team contacts them.

## Audit criteria

Check:
- Are leads coming from third-party vendors?
- Does the CRM store consent source and timestamp?
- Is the original landing page/disclosure archived?
- Is there a DNC/suppression check?
- Are phone/SMS/email rules separated?
- Can the buyer reconstruct the chain for a disputed lead?

## Sprint deliverables

- Lead source inventory.
- Consent field standardization.
- CRM consent badge.
- Landing-page screenshot/archive step.
- Vendor/sub-vendor chain fields.
- DNC/suppression workflow.
- Outbound gating logic.
- Complaint lookup dashboard.
- Weekly lead-source risk report.
- Loom handoff and SOP.

## Outreach angle

> If a lead complains, can your team prove exactly where that lead came from, what consent text they saw, and why your rep was allowed to call or text? I build that evidence chain inside the CRM.

## Risks

- Buyers may expect legal advice.
- Some lead sellers intentionally avoid visibility.
- Tooling varies heavily by CRM and lead vendor.
