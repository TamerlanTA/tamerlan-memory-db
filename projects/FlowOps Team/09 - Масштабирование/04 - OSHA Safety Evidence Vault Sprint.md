## Content

## Status

**PROMISING**, especially for contractors and stone/countertop fabrication. FlowOps should not sell safety consulting. The wedge is a lightweight evidence vault that helps operators keep training, inspections, reminders, and abatement proof organized.

## Buyer

- roofing contractors;
- general contractors;
- subcontractor-heavy construction firms;
- countertop and engineered-stone fabricators;
- small manufacturing shops with safety logs.

Best economic buyer: owner, operations manager, safety coordinator.

## Unfairgap

Small operators may have real safety work happening informally, but no reliable evidence chain:
- missing training records;
- no toolbox talk history;
- no photo proof of fixes;
- no exposure-control task reminders;
- no inspection log;
- no abatement deadline tracking.

When a violation or incident happens, the company cannot show a clean record.

## What FlowOps sells

**Safety Evidence Vault Sprint**

FlowOps builds a simple mobile-first system for safety tasks, training records, inspection logs, photo evidence, and reminder workflows.

## Audit criteria

Check:
- How are safety trainings logged?
- Are toolbox talks recorded?
- Are PPE and site checks captured with photos?
- Are silica/fall-protection tasks tracked?
- Are incident notes centralized?
- Are abatement or correction deadlines visible?
- Does management get reminders?

## Sprint deliverables

- Airtable/Notion safety evidence base.
- Mobile form for toolbox talks and site checks.
- Photo upload workflow.
- Employee/training record table.
- Reminder automation for recurring safety tasks.
- Abatement/deadline tracker.
- Weekly owner summary.
- Loom handoff and SOP.

## Pricing

- Sprint: **$1,500-$5,000**.
- Monthly support: **$500-$2,000/month**.

## Outreach angle

> Most small contractors do safety work, but the proof is scattered across texts, paper, photos, and memory. I build a lightweight evidence vault so training, inspections, and corrective actions are easy to prove later.

## Risks

- Needs careful positioning: not legal advice, not safety certification.
- Safety consultants and EHS platforms already exist.
- Some customers may only care after a violation.

## First experiment

Target:
- stone/countertop fabricators;
- roofing companies;
- contractors mentioning safety certifications or hiring safety roles.

Goal:
- 20 targeted audits/research profiles;
- 5 conversations;
