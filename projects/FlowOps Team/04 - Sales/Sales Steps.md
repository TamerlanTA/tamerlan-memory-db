# 🏆 Sales Steps — 7-Step Process

#flowops #sales #process

> [!abstract] The FlowOps Sales Framework
> Every closed deal follows this 7-step path. Skip steps and you lose deals. Rush the pitch before establishing pain, and you'll always compete on price. Follow the sequence and closing becomes natural.

---

## Step 1 — Find the Signal

**What:** Identify a prospect who has expressed a clear pain, is actively looking for help, or whose business has a visible automation gap.

**Sources:**
- Upwork job post (active buying intent) → [[Pipeline A — Upwork Radar]]
- LinkedIn pain post (passive intent) → [[Pipeline B — LinkedIn Pain Radar]]
- Website audit finding (cold outreach) → [[Pipeline C — Website Audit Generator]]
- Referral or inbound

**Output:** Qualified prospect in CRM with pain hypothesis

> [!tip] Quality over Quantity
> One well-researched prospect outperforms 50 cold blasts. Know their niche, their likely pain, and their tech stack before you reach out.

---

## Step 2 — The Opener

**What:** First contact message. Hyper-personalized. References their specific situation. No pitch. One ask: a response.

**Principles:**
- Lead with THEIR pain, not your services
- Reference something specific (their post, their website, their job listing)
- Make the CTA tiny: "Would it be useful?" / "Worth a quick look?"
- Offer the Free Audit as a no-risk entry point

**Templates:** → [[Message Templates]]

---

## Step 3 — Pre-Call Research

**What:** Before the discovery call, do 10 minutes of research.

**Research checklist:**
- [ ] Company website — what do they sell, how do they get leads?
- [ ] LinkedIn — their role, how long at company, any posts about ops?
- [ ] Tech stack (BuiltWith/Wappalyzer) — CRM, email, booking tools
- [ ] Reviews (Google, Trustpilot) — any patterns of operational complaints?
- [ ] Any demos in [[Pipeline D — Demo Library]] relevant to their niche?

---

## Step 4 — Discovery Call

**What:** A 20–30 minute call focused 80% on diagnosing their pain, 20% on positioning your solution.

**Goal:** Understand their situation deeply enough to make a specific, ROI-backed proposal.

**Script:** → [[Discovery Call Script]]

**Key questions:**
1. What's the biggest bottleneck in your ops right now?
2. What does that cost you per month (time + money)?
3. What have you tried to fix it?
4. What does success look like in 90 days?

> [!warning] Do Not Pitch on the Discovery Call
> The discovery call is for diagnosing, not selling. End with: "Based on what you've shared, I think I know exactly how to help. Let me put together a short proposal and send it over today."

---

## Step 5 — The Proposal

**What:** A concise, specific proposal that shows you understood their problem and maps it to a clear solution with ROI.

**Structure:**
```
1. Their Situation (2–3 sentences — mirror their pain back)
2. The Solution (what you'll build, how it works)
3. What They Get (deliverables)
4. The Investment (price + what's included)
5. The ROI (time or money saved vs. cost)
6. Next Step (simple CTA — "Reply yes to get started")
```

> [!tip] Proposal Length
> 1 page or less. If you need more than that, you haven't simplified enough. Complexity kills deals.

---

## Step 6 — Handle Objections

**"It's too expensive"**
> "Totally fair. Let me show you the math. [ROI calculation]. Most clients recoup the investment in [timeframe]. If that doesn't happen, I'll work for free until it does — that's the confidence I have in this."

**"Can you do it cheaper?"**
> "I can build a smaller version — here's what that looks like. But the full version is what gets you to [outcome]. What's your priority — budget or the full result?"

**"I need to think about it"**
> "Of course. What specific part are you weighing? I want to make sure you have everything you need to decide."

**"Can I hire someone in-house instead?"**
> "Absolutely. A full-time ops person is $4–6K/month plus benefits. We're [price]/month — and we start immediately. But if in-house is right for your stage, I'd tell you that too."

---

## Step 7 — Close and Onboard

**What:** Get the signed agreement and first payment. Begin onboarding.

**Close mechanics:**
- Use a simple contract (1-page or Google Doc — keep it frictionless)
- Accept payment upfront (50% deposit or full payment for Sprints)
- Send onboarding form within 1 hour of payment
- Schedule kickoff call within 48 hours

**Onboarding form collects:**
- Access to tools (CRM, email, lead sources)
- Current process walkthrough (Loom from them)
- Goals and KPIs for the sprint
- Primary contact + communication preference
