# 💰 How to Sell Price

#flowops #sales #pricing #roi

> [!quote] The Core Principle
> "We don't talk about price until they've told us what the problem is costing them. Price is always revealed relative to the ROI — never in isolation."

---

## The ROI Formula

```
ROI = (Value Created or Recovered) ÷ (Our Fee) × 100

Target: Every proposal should show ≥ 3x ROI minimum
Strong proposals show 5–10x ROI
```

---

## The Four ROI Categories

### 1. Time Saved (Most Common)

> [!example] Formula
> Hours saved per week × Hourly labor cost × 4.3 weeks = Monthly savings

**Example:**
- Client's team spends 8 hrs/week on manual data entry
- Average team member cost: $25/hr
- Monthly savings: 8 × $25 × 4.3 = **$860/month**
- Our Sprint: $1,500 → **Pays back in 1.7 months, then pure savings**

---

### 2. Revenue Recovered (Lead-Related Systems)

> [!example] Formula
> Current leads/month × (New response rate % − Old response rate %) × Close rate × Average deal value = Monthly revenue recovered

**Example:**
- 40 leads/month, currently 20% response rate → 8 clients
- With Speed-to-Lead: 70% response rate → 28 clients
- Close rate: 25%
- Average deal: $800
- New closed deals: 28 × 25% = 7 → was 2 (8 × 25%)
- Extra 5 clients × $800 = **$4,000/month recovered**
- Our system: $1,500 → **ROI in first week**

---

### 3. Cost of Error Reduction

> [!example] Formula
> Average cost per error × Errors per month eliminated = Monthly savings

**Example:**
- Data entry errors cause wrong orders 5x/month
- Each error costs $200 to fix (staff time + refund risk)
- 5 × $200 = **$1,000/month in error costs eliminated**

---

### 4. Opportunity Cost (What Else Could They Do With the Time)

> [!example] Framing
> "You spend 10 hours a week on admin. If you used those 10 hours on sales/client work at your billable rate of $200/hr, that's $2,000/week you're leaving on the table. Our system frees that up permanently for $1,500."

## How to Present Price

### Never Quote Price First

Always follow this sequence:
1. Diagnose the pain ✓
2. Get THEM to quantify the cost ✓
3. Paint the outcome ✓
4. THEN present the price

> "So we've established this is costing you roughly [their number] per month. The system I'd build to fix this costs [price]. That means you recoup the investment in [X weeks/months], and after that it's pure savings. Does that math work for your situation?"

---

### The Price Anchor Technique

Always present 3 tiers. The middle tier is what you actually want to sell.

| Tier | Price | Effect |
|------|-------|--------|
| Basic | $750 | Makes middle look full-featured |
| **Standard** | **$1,500** | **Your target — best value** |
| Premium | $2,500 | Makes standard look affordable |

> [!tip] Anchoring Psychology
> Most prospects self-select into the middle option. By starting high (Premium), the Standard feels like a bargain. By having a Basic, you show flexibility.

---

### Reframing Common Objections

**"$1,500 is a lot of money"**
> "I get it. Let me put it differently: you told me this issue costs you $X/month. At $1,500 once, you're paying for [X months] of the problem to stop forever. After that, it runs for free. Does that frame it differently?"

**"Can you do it for $500?"**
> "At $500 I can do [specific smaller scope]. That solves part of the problem. The full solution that gets you to [outcome] is $1,500. Which matters more to you right now — the budget or the full result?"

**"We don't have budget right now"**
> "I understand. What would it take for budget to be available? Because if the system saves you $X/month, you actually lose $X every month we wait. Is there a way to make this happen now?"

## ROI Examples by Offer

### Speed-to-Lead System — $1,500

> "You're spending $3,000/month on ads and closing 10% of leads. We'll push that to 35%. At your average deal of $2,000, that's [calc]. Investment pays back in week 1."

### Ops Automation Sprint — $1,500

> "Your team spends 15 hrs/week on data entry. At $30/hr average, that's $1,935/month. Our sprint costs $1,500. You're ahead by month 2, and it saves $1,900/month forever after."

### AI Chatbot — $2,500

> "You're losing 60% of your website leads after hours. You get 200 visitors/month, 5% currently convert. Chatbot pushes that to 15%. At your average booking value of $500, that's [calc]/month extra. ROI in first month."

### Retainer — $1,000/mo

> "Over the past sprint, we saved you X hours/month. With the retainer, we'll tackle 2 more systems per month. Each one saves a similar amount. By month 3, you'll have 3 systems running that together save $4–5K/month. The retainer costs $1K. Does that math work?"
