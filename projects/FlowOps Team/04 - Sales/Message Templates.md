# ✉️ Message Templates

#flowops #sales #templates #outreach

> [!abstract] Usage Guide
> Every template below is a **starting framework** — always personalize the bracketed sections before sending. The more specific the reference to THEIR situation, the higher the response rate.

---

## 1. Upwork Proposal Template

**Length target:** 120–160 words

---

> **[HOOK — Reference their specific situation, 1 sentence]**
> I noticed you're building a [Speed-to-Lead / chatbot / ops automation] system for a [niche] business — this is exactly the kind of project I specialize in.
>
> **[REFRAME — Show deeper understanding]**
> Most [niche] businesses I've worked with struggle not just with [surface problem] but with [deeper issue they may not have named] — and that's usually where the real time/money loss is.
>
> **[PROOF — Specific and brief]**
> I recently built a [similar system] for a [similar client] that [specific measurable result]. I can share the Loom if it's useful.
>
> **[SOLUTION FIT]**
> For your project, I'd approach this with [tool/method] — here's why: [1 sentence of reasoning].
>
> **[CTA]**
> Happy to jump on a 15-minute call to see if this is a fit — or I can send a short audit of your setup first.
>
> [Your name]

---

**Variations by offer type:**

*Speed-to-Lead:*
> "I build lead-response systems that get back to every inbound lead in under 60 seconds. For [niche], this typically recovers [X%] of leads that currently go cold."

*Ops Automation:*
> "For [niche] businesses, the biggest win is usually automating [specific manual task]. I've built this for similar teams and it consistently saves 8–15 hrs/week."

*Chatbot:*
> "I build AI chatbots trained on your business that qualify leads, answer FAQs, and book calls 24/7. Particularly high-impact for [niche] where [typical pain]."

## 2. LinkedIn DM Templates

### Template A — Pain Post Response

**Context:** They posted about a struggle on LinkedIn

> "Hey [Name] — saw your post about [specific thing they said]. That's exactly the kind of problem I work on for [their type of business].
>
> We've fixed this for [similar company type] — usually takes [timeframe] and saves [X hrs/week or $X/mo]. Would it be useful if I sent you a quick Loom showing how it works?"

---

### Template B — After Connecting (No Prior Post)

**Context:** Connected based on their profile/role

> "Hey [Name], appreciate the connect.
>
> I noticed you're at [Company] — based on what you do, I'd guess [specific operational challenge] is probably a recurring headache.
>
> I work with [their type of business] on exactly that. Not pitching anything — just wanted to put it on your radar. If it ever becomes relevant, happy to show you what we've built."

---

### Template C — Follow-Up (No Reply, Day 3)

> "Hey [Name], just bumping this in case it got buried.
>
> I put together a quick [2-minute Loom / short audit] for [their company] showing specifically what I'd do about [pain]. No obligation, just thought it might be useful.
>
> [Link or offer to share]"

## 3. Cold Email Templates

### Template A — Website Audit Opener

**Subject:** "Quick audit of [Company] — 3 things I noticed"

> Hi [Name],
>
> I ran [Company]'s website through our automation audit and noticed a few things that are probably costing you leads each week.
>
> Specifically, [ONE specific finding — e.g., "your contact form doesn't trigger an instant response, which means leads are waiting hours to hear back"].
>
> I put together a 2-minute Loom showing what that looks like and what a fix would cost. Worth sending over?
>
> — [Your Name]
> [Title] | FlowOps

---

### Template B — Niche Pain Opener

**Subject:** "[Their niche] businesses usually lose [X] leads a week — here's why"

> Hi [Name],
>
> Most [niche] businesses I talk to are losing [specific type of lead] not because they lack leads, but because [specific reason — slow response / no follow-up / no 24/7 coverage].
>
> I've built systems that fix exactly this for [similar businesses]. [Specific result].
>
> Would a 15-minute call be worthwhile? I'd walk you through what this looks like for your setup specifically.
>
> — [Your Name]

---

### Template C — Hiring Signal Trigger

**Subject:** "Noticed [Company] is hiring for [ops role]"

> Hi [Name],
>
> I saw [Company] is looking for a [Operations Manager / Data Entry / CRM Admin].
>
> Depending on what that person will be doing, there's a chance automation could handle a significant portion of it — at a fraction of the cost, and starting this week.
>
> Not trying to talk you out of hiring — just wanted to flag it in case it's worth a 15-minute conversation before you commit to a salary.
>
> — [Your Name]

## 4. Retainer Pitch Template

**Context:** Post-Sprint, system is live, client is happy

**Message or email:**

> "Hey [Name], glad [the system] is running well!
>
> While building it, I noticed [2–3 other problems you spotted]. These are exactly the kinds of things that tend to get worse as your business grows.
>
> A lot of my clients at this stage keep me on as their ongoing automation partner — I fix edge cases, build new workflows as things change, and make sure everything keeps running. Usually 1–3 new automations a month.
>
> It's [price]/month — we can start with one month and see how it goes.
>
> Would that be useful?"

## 📏 Template Performance Rules

> [!info] Principles That Improve Response Rates
> 1. **First sentence must reference THEM** — not you, not your services
> 2. **One ask per message** — never ask multiple questions
> 3. **Under 150 words** for cold outreach
> 4. **No attachments on first contact** — only offer to share on request
> 5. **Plain text beats designed emails** — for cold outreach especially
> 6. **Follow up exactly twice** — Day 3 and Day 7. Then move on.
