- Deck frames the offer as a business system: trend monitoring → archive matching → video intelligence → automatic clipping → review queue → YouTube publishing.

## Key findings
- The project should not be sold as a simple Make/n8n automation; the hard part is video understanding, clip quality, speaker/context detection, and trend relevance.
- Recommended sales path:
  - MVP: 2–3 weeks, $1,500–$2,500 with review queue and manual approval.
  - Production: 4–6 weeks, $4,000–$7,000+ with video/audio analysis, smart crop, ranking, upload/schedule.
  - Autonomous: 6–10 weeks, $8,000–$15,000+ with autoposting, brand safety, anti-duplicate memory, analytics loop, monitoring.
- Strongest buyer argument: convert an existing content archive into daily trend-responsive Shorts while saving 80–140 hours/month of research, editing, and publishing labor.

## Blockers
- Exact ROI depends on channel economics: current views, monetization, sponsor value, team hourly costs, and volume of source videos.
- Full autoposting should be delayed until the system has passed quality checks on 50–100 candidate clips.

## Next steps
- Use the presentation in client conversation to sell an MVP first, not full autonomy from day one.
- Ask the client for 10–20 source videos, target topics, examples of good/bad clips, and channel publishing constraints.
- If client agrees, turn MVP into a scoped proposal with deliverables, milestones, review process, and acceptance criteria.
