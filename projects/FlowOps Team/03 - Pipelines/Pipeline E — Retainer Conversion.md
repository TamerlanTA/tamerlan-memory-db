# 🔄 Pipeline E — Retainer Conversion

#flowops #pipeline #retainer #conversion

> [!abstract] Goal
> Convert Sprint clients into Long-Term Retainer clients. This is the highest-leverage move in the entire FlowOps system — it turns one-time revenue into predictable MRR without additional acquisition cost.

---

## ⏰ When to Pitch the Retainer

> [!warning] Timing is Everything
> **Never** pitch the retainer before the Sprint is delivered. **Never** pitch it cold.
> The perfect moment is **7–14 days after Sprint delivery**, once the client has seen the system working.

**Trigger signals that it's time:**
- Client messaged you saying the system is working well
- Client asked "can we do something similar for X?"
- Sprint delivered, client confirmed it's live and running
- You've identified 2+ additional problems in their workflow
- Client has been responsive and engaged post-delivery

---

## 🗣️ The Retainer Conversion Script

### Opening (on a call or in a message):

> "Hey [Name], glad the [Sprint system] is running smoothly. I wanted to check in because while building it, I noticed a few other things that are probably costing you [time/leads/money].
>
> Things like: [Problem 2], [Problem 3], and eventually [Problem 4].
>
> The way a lot of my clients handle this is by keeping me on for a few hours a month — I fix issues, add new automations as they come up, and basically become your ongoing automation partner.
>
> It's [price]/month and we can stop anytime. Want me to walk you through what that looks like?"

---

### If They Ask "What Would You Actually Do?"

> "Every month, I'd:
> 1. Keep your existing workflows running and fix any issues
> 2. Build at least [1–2] new automations based on your current priorities
> 3. Give you a short monthly report on what's working
> 4. Be available async if something breaks or you need a quick tweak
>
> Basically — you stop thinking about automation and I own that for you."

---

### Handling "Can I Just Call You When I Need Something?"

> "Totally fair question. The reason I offer a monthly structure instead is that it keeps me prioritizing your projects — ad hoc requests go to the back of the queue. With a retainer, you're always first. But I get it if the timing isn't right — just let me know when it is."

## 🪜 Tier Details

| Tier | Price | What's Included |
|------|-------|----------------|
| **Starter** | $500/mo | Monitor existing workflows, fix issues, 1 new automation/mo, 30-min check-in call |
| **Growth** | $1,000/mo | 2–3 new automations/mo, priority async support, monthly report, strategy recommendations |
| **Partner** | $2,000+/mo | 4+ systems/mo, dedicated Slack channel, full ops roadmap ownership, team training |

> [!tip] Anchor Up
> Always present all 3 tiers. Most clients choose the middle. Presenting only the lowest tier leaves money on the table.

## 📊 Retainer Economics

```
Goal: 5 retainer clients at $1,000/month = $5,000 MRR

Sprint conversion rate target: 50–60%
(If you close 2 Sprints/month, expect 1 retainer/month)

After 6 months at this pace:
Month 1: $0 MRR (Sprints only)
Month 2: $1,000 MRR (1 retainer)
Month 3: $2,000 MRR
Month 4: $3,000 MRR
Month 5: $4,000 MRR
Month 6: $5,000 MRR + Sprints
```

---

## 🔁 Retainer Renewal Process

Every 30 days:
1. Send a brief monthly summary (2–3 key wins this month)
2. Share the roadmap for next month (2–3 planned automations)
3. Ask if priorities have shifted
4. Invoice

> [!success] The Renewal Secret
> The summary email is the most important retention tool. Clients who see a concrete "here's what I built for you this month" rarely cancel. Clients who don't hear from you do.
